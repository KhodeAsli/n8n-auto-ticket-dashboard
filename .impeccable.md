# Design Context - IT Helpdesk Dashboard

> **Last Updated**: 2026-03-16
> **Status**: Active

---

## Users

**Mixed Audience** - Both IT management and frontline support staff use this dashboard:

- **IT Managers / Team Leads**: View team performance KPIs, outlier detection, staff rankings on desktop
- **IT Support Staff**: Track their own tickets, response times, and pending work items
- **Context**: Thai enterprise environment - users speak Thai, expect familiar UI patterns

**Jobs to be done**:
- Monitor ticket volume and resolution times
- Identify performance outliers (statistical anomaly detection)
- Track staff performance rankings
- Search and filter tickets efficiently

---

## Brand Personality

**Three words**: Professional, Trustworthy, Clear

**Voice & Tone**:
- **Confident**: The dashboard should inspire trust in the data it presents
- **Enterprise-grade**: Feels reliable and suitable for business operations
- **Approachable**: Despite being professional, it should feel easy to use for all staff levels
- **Data-first**: Information is the priority, decoration is secondary

---

## Aesthetic Direction

**Visual Tone**: Minimal, refined, sophisticated - inspired by Linear / Vercel aesthetic

**Key Characteristics**:
- **Typography-driven**: Excellent type hierarchy, refined font choices (NOT Inter/Roboto/system defaults)
- **Subtle colors**: Refined palette with purposeful accents, not loud or saturated
- **Generous whitespace**: Breathing room, not cramped or cluttered
- **Subtle gradients**: Very subtle, purposeful use - not prominent gradient text or backgrounds
- **Refined details**: Small touches that show care and intentionality

**Anti-References** (What to avoid):
- Dark mode with glowing neon accents
- Gradient text on headings or metrics
- Glassmorphism everywhere
- Generic AI slop aesthetics from 2024-2025
- Bootstrap-like corporate blue
- Over-designed with distracting animations

**Color Direction**:
- Move away from standard Tailwind colors (gray-50, blue-500, etc.)
- Consider more sophisticated neutrals tinted toward a hue
- Purposeful accent colors that guide attention, not decorate
- Tint neutrals toward brand color for subconscious cohesion

**Typography Direction**:
- Choose distinctive, beautiful fonts - avoid overused ones
- Display font for headings with character
- Body font that's refined and readable
- Modular type scale with fluid sizing (clamp)
- Clear visual hierarchy through size, weight, and spacing

---

## Design Principles

1. **Data Clarity First**: Information must be instantly scannable and understandable
2. **Professional Polish**: Every detail should feel intentional and refined
3. **Reduced Cognitive Load**: Don't make users think harder than necessary
4. **Responsive Without Amputation**: Adapt interfaces for mobile, don't hide functionality
5. **Subtle Motion**: Use motion purposefully for feedback, not decoration
